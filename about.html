Our mission

Our Mission

The Garden School is an initiative of The Garden Collective, a 501(c)(3) based in Hamden, Connecticut. We offer GED support, mentorship, meditation, weekly dinners, and creative workshops for young people moving through instability, returning from detention, or feeling disconnected from school. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

We start from the belief that learning opens up when students feel safe, welcomed, and genuinely seen. Our model is home-based, relational, and attentive to trauma. Education happens in community — at tables, in conversation, and through creative and practical work that builds confidence and autonomy. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Our approach

How We Hold the Space

Our approach weaves home, relationship, nervous system care, and community into every part of the program. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Home-Based

Learning in a house-like setting lowers the temperature of school anxiety, softens institutional barriers, and helps students feel physically and emotionally safer. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Relational

Trust and connection come first. Mentorship, consistency, and showing up over time are at the center of how we teach and how we build community. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Trauma-Aware

We recognize the impact of trauma on learning. Our pacing, expectations, and environment are designed to make room for healing alongside academic work. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Community-Based

Growth happens through shared meals, creative practice, and collective care — not in isolation. Students, mentors, and neighbors shape the space together. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

About The Garden Collective

The Ecosystem Around The Garden School

The Garden Collective is a 501(c)(3) focused on building spaces of education, art, healing, and mutual aid. Our work sits at the crossroads of abolition, community care, and transformative justice. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

The Garden School is our flagship education project. Alongside it, we are growing workshops, artist residencies, and partnerships across Greater New Haven to support many ways of learning and making life together. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}
