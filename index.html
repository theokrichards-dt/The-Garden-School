About The Garden School

The Garden School is an initiative of The Garden Collective, a 501(c)(3) based in Hamden, Connecticut. We offer accessible GED instruction, mentorship, meditation, weekly community dinners, and creative workshops for youth navigating instability, reentry, or school disengagement.

Our model is home-based, relational, and trauma-aware. We believe education becomes possible when young people feel safe, welcomed, supported, and seen. Learning happens in community — around tables, in conversation, and through the creative and practical skills that build confidence and autonomy.

We meet students where they are, pacing our teaching to real lives and nervous systems instead of forcing them to fit inflexible institutional schedules. The Garden School lives inside a larger ecosystem of dinners, residencies, and workshops at The Garden Collective, where learning, art, and rest are treated as shared responsibilities.

Programs

What We Offer

Our programming weaves together academic support, nervous system regulation, and hands-on creative practice. Students can move between offerings depending on what they need most in a given season.

GED Instruction

Small-group and one-on-one learning, paced to each student’s needs. We focus on building confidence, reducing school-related anxiety, and supporting long-term academic and career goals.

Community & Mentorship

Weekly dinners, meditation sessions taught by community leaders, and ongoing relational support. We create a consistent, grounding space where students can breathe, reset, and reconnect.

Creative & Vocational Skills

Workshops in cooking, sewing, carpentry, art-making, and other rotating skills taught by local artists and mentors. Students learn practical abilities while discovering new interests and strengths.

Support The Garden School

The Garden School operates entirely through community support. Donations keep our classes free, fund workshop materials, provide meals, and help us expand our reach to more young people who need a soft place to land.

We are a registered 501(c)(3), and all contributions are tax-deductible.

Your support directly covers things like:

  • Art, sewing, and carpentry materials.
